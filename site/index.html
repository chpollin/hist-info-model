Five scholarly tasks. Real historical examples. The properties of historical information emerge from the experience — not from labels.

P1

Temporal Irreversibility

The described states of affairs cannot be re-observed.

P2

Trace Mediation

All access to the referent runs exclusively through traces.

P3

Interpretive Constitution

Historical information does not exist independently of interpretation.

These three primitives are analytically distinct and each necessary. From them, eleven derived properties and twenty-four formal requirements follow. The visualizations below let you experience what these properties mean in practice — through real historical data, not abstract diagrams.

Context

About This Project

Research Prototype

This website makes the properties of historical information experienceable through interactive visualizations of real historical data. 24 formal requirements emerge from three primitives — and from the scholarly tasks of reading, tracing, comparing, reconstructing, and reflecting.

Promptotype

This website is itself a Promptotype — built using the Promptotyping method, an iterative context-engineering approach for data-driven research artifacts with frontier LLMs. The method amplifies domain expertise asymmetrically.

Theory

The formalized theory of historical information is based on three irreducible primitives (temporal irreversibility, trace mediation, interpretive constitution) from which eleven properties and twenty-four requirements are derived.
